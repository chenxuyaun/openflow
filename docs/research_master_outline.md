# OpenFlow Research Master Outline

## Purpose

Create a full-spectrum research foundation for OpenFlow so future sessions and
team members can work from files rather than from implicit chat memory.

## Source Families

### Internal

- Current and historical chat transcripts
- README, architecture notes, tests, and code
- Git history, milestone changes, and commit intent
- Project-specific decisions and unresolved questions

### External

- Competitor and adjacent product references
- Agent workflow and handoff governance patterns
- Knowledge management and traceability practices
- Planning and review loop design references

## Theme Index

- Product narrative
- Role orchestration
- Session isolation
- Handoff governance
- Knowledge indexing
- Execution tracking
- Review and replanning

## Research Rules

- Every source becomes a structured knowledge item
- Every knowledge item declares themes and reliability
- Product decisions must cite supporting knowledge items
- Conflicts remain visible until resolved in the decision registry

## Planned Outputs

- `docs/product_hook.md`
- `docs/master_prd.md`
- `docs/decision_registry.json`
- `docs/knowledge_index.json`
- `docs/workflow_blueprint.json`

## Normalization Standard

Each research entry should answer:

- What is the source and why does it matter?
- Which product themes does it support or challenge?
- What decision does it influence?
- Is it adopted, rejected, or deferred for V1?
